# RIDE-IT-Driver-Engagement-Supply-Analysis

This project provides a comprehensive data-driven evaluation of driver performance, market trends, and operational efficiency for RIDE IT, a multi-service mobility platform. By integrating driver profiles with activity logs, this analysis identifies critical growth opportunities and engagement strategies for the European market.  

## 📊 Project Overview

The Supply Product team required an analytical deep-dive to optimize the driver experience and platform reliability.  Success Monitoring: Proposed key metrics to track engagement trends over time.  Behavioral Identification: Isolated factors that correlate with high-performing "Gold Status" drivers.  Actionable Strategy: Developed data-backed initiatives for product and business growth.  

## 💡 Key Insights & Analytics

1. Fleet Composition & Market GapMarket Scale: Analyzed 36,972 unique drivers across Germany (DE) and Spain (ES).  Service Dominance: 84.72% of the fleet operates as TAXI, while Private Hire Vehicles (PHV) make up 15.28%.  Geographic Disparity: Spain (ES) is currently limited to TAXI services only, representing a massive untapped market for PHV expansion.

2. Demand Dynamics & EfficiencyPeak Pressure: Offer volume surges on Wednesdays and Saturdays, with both days exceeding 4.5 million offers.  Supply Bottlenecks: Saturday conversion efficiency drops to ~20% (compared to ~28% on Tuesdays), indicating that high demand is overwhelming current driver supply.  Pandemic Recovery: A sharp decline in rides was recorded from 2019 (28.46%) to 2020 (8.79%), highlighting the volatility of global events.

3. Advanced Engagement MetricsMarketing Uplift: Drivers opted into marketing completed 3.46 rides on average, compared to just 2.51 for those who opted out.Demand Pressure: Saturday is the most taxing day for the fleet, with 142.0 offers per active driver.Churn Risk: The 30-day churn rate stands at 37.86%, signaling a need for immediate retention strategies.

## 🛠 Tech Stack & Methodology

Python (Pandas):Executed data cleaning, complex merging of datasets, and feature engineering.

Visualization (Seaborn/Matplotlib): Engineered stakeholder-ready Heatmaps, Line Charts, and Correlation Matrices.  

Statistical Analysis: Performed correlation tests finding a 0.95 link between Bookings and Rides, while Driver Ratings showed no significant impact on ride volume.  

## 🚀 Strategic Recommendations

Regional Expansion: Prioritize recruitment in Spain (ES) to match German driver density and pilot PHV services.  

Monetization & Policy: Implement stricter passenger cancellation fees, especially during Saturday peaks, to reduce friction and protect driver revenue.  

Retention Incentives: Use the 71% marketing opt-in rate to push weekend bonuses and "Gold Status" rewards to mitigate the 37% churn rate.  

Quality Management: Targeted behavioral coaching for the bottom 10 drivers to maintain the fleet's 99% "Top" rating status.
